Famous Attractions

Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia is one of Istanbul’s most iconic landmarks, reflecting the city’s rich and layered history. Originally built as a Byzantine cathedral in the 6th century, it later became an Ottoman mosque and is now a mosque and museum. Its massive dome and stunning mosaics draw visitors from around the world.

Blue Mosque

The Blue Mosque, also known as Sultan Ahmed Mosque, is one of Istanbul’s most iconic landmarks. Built in the early 17th century, it’s famous for its grand domes, six minarets, and stunning blue Iznik tiles inside. The mosque remains an active place of worship and a major tourist attraction.

Taksim Square

Taksim Square is the heart of modern Istanbul, known for its lively atmosphere, shops, hotels, and cultural landmarks. It’s a central gathering place for locals and tourists, surrounded by restaurants and historic sites. Taksim also serves as a hub for public events, celebrations, and political demonstrations, symbolizing Istanbul’s dynamic spirit.

Topkapi Palace

Topkapı Palace is one of Istanbul’s most famous landmarks and a symbol of Ottoman power and splendor. Built in the 15th century by Sultan Mehmed II, it served as the residence of Ottoman sultans for centuries. Today, it’s a museum showcasing imperial treasures, stunning architecture, and breathtaking views of the Bosphorus.

The Ortaköy Mosque

Ortaköy Mosque is a stunning example of Ottoman Baroque architecture, located on the shores of the Bosphorus in Istanbul. Built in the 19th century, it’s a beloved landmark known for its elegant design and scenic setting, drawing visitors for both its beauty and its vibrant surrounding neighborhood.

Maiden's Tower

The Maiden’s Tower is a legendary landmark standing on a small islet in the Bosphorus near Istanbul’s shores. With a history dating back centuries, it has served as a lighthouse, defense tower, and customs station. Today, it captivates visitors with its romantic stories, beautiful views, and charming café.
